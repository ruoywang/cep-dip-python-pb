The 1-D closure route: theory, tests, and where it lands

Everything measured about replacing the per-structure 3-D nonlinear PB solve with a plane-averaged 1-D solve: why the naive reduction fails by construction, the two closure quantities that fix it, how accurately each must be known, and the resulting architecture — a differentiable ~0.03 s solvent response for training and MD alike.

1Theory: from VASPsol++ to the anchored 1-D closure

Notation and equation numbers follow the VASPsol++ paper (Islam, Khezeli, Ringe & Plaisance, J. Chem. Phys. 159, 234117 (2023)). ⟨·⟩ denotes the lateral (xy) plane average.

(a) The 3-D model. Cavities are built from the solute electron density ne(r) through the shape function [Eqs. (1)–(3)], chained into the dielectric and ionic cavities Sdiel{ne}(r), Sion{ne}(r):

Eqs. (1)–(2)S{n}(r)=12erfc(ln(n/nc)σ2)

The ground state solves the nonlinear Poisson–Boltzmann equation [Eq. (55)] with the bound and ionic charges [Eqs. (56), (57)]:

Eqs. (55)–(57)−ε04π∇2φ=ρsol(r)+ρb{φ}+ρion{φ}ρb=−nmol(wb∗∇·(Sdielp)),ρion=nmaxSionρ~ion

The molecular dipole p responds locally and nonlinearly to the local field ℰ [Eqs. (27), (28), (32), (34)], and the ions are Boltzmann-like with lattice saturation [Eqs. (51), (53)]:

Eqs. (27), (28)+(32), (34)ℰ=−(wb∗∇φ)+4πε0αsicpp=ε04π[αrot0grot(βpmolℰ)+αpol]ℰgrot(x)=3x2(xcothx−1)
Eqs. (51), (53)ρ~ion=−ε04παionbgion(βzφ)φgion(x)=1xsinhx1−θionb+θionbcoshx

Note what is absent: any offset. p vanishes wherever ℰ vanishes, point by point — the constitutive law is local, universal, and needs no anchor, because the lateral structure is fully resolved.

(b) Exact reduction. Plane-averaging Eq. (55) is exact — lateral derivatives average to zero by periodicity. With the polarization density P ≡ nmol Sdiel p:

exact, ⟨Eq. (55)⟩−ε04πd2⟨φ⟩dz2=⟨ρsol⟩+⟨ρb⟩+⟨ρion⟩⟨ρb⟩=−ddz(wb∗⟨Pz⟩)

Because ⟨ρb⟩ is a total derivative of a periodic profile, ∫⟨ρb⟩dz = 0 identically — the bound-charge sum rule survives any closure (verified to 10⁻¹⁶ for all methods in §3). What the reduction does not supply is ⟨Pz⟩(z) itself: that is the closure problem.

(c) Why no dielectric function ε(z) exists. In the low-field limit the constitutive law becomes a pointwise product:

low-field limitPz(r)=ε04πχ(r)ℰz(r)χ≡flocnmolSdiel(αrot0grot+αpol)
ℰ here is the self-interaction-free field −(wb∗∇φ) — the one the 1-D solver sees. In §(a) the dipole responds to the local field, which contains its own reaction term (4π/ε₀αsic) p; eliminating p from that self-consistency at constant polarizability (the low-field step) gives the local-field factor floc = (1 − α/αsic)−1 with α ≡ α⁰rotgrot+αpol [Eq. (41)]. Strictly grot → 1 in this limit — the zero-field slope A(z) of §(d) takes exactly that form.

Averaging the product splits identically:

covariance identity⟨Pz⟩=ε04π[⟨χ⟩⟨ℰz⟩+⟨δχ·δℰz⟩],δX≡X−⟨X⟩

The covariance term is not multiplicative in ⟨ℰz⟩: measured interface planes have ⟨ℰz⟩ = 0 with ⟨Pz⟩ ≠ 0 (χ and ℰ are laterally correlated where the cavity weaves around atoms), so no ε(z) can reproduce the averaged physics — the 3-D law loses its offset-free form under coarse-graining.

(d) The affine closure and the anchor. The effective 1-D constitutive law therefore carries an inhomogeneous term:

affine closure⟨Pz⟩(z)=A(z)⟨ℰz⟩(z)+Poff(z)A(z)≡ε04πflocnmol⟨Sdiel⟩(z)(αrot0+αpol)
zero-field formula; the bulk limit reproduces εb [Eqs. (42)–(43)]
anchorPoff(z)≡⟨Pz⟩ref−A(z)⟨ℰz⟩ref
anchor ≡ the pair (⟨ℰz⟩ref, ⟨Pz⟩ref) of one converged 3-D solution

Two consequences follow directly. Anchor identity: substituting back at the reference state, A ⟨ℰ⟩ref + Poff = ⟨P⟩ref for any A — the split between slope and offset is a convention at the anchor; changing A merely reapportions Poff. Error structure: away from the anchor, ⟨P⟩ = ⟨P⟩ref + A·(⟨ℰ⟩−⟨ℰ⟩ref), so an anchor error is zeroth order (a constant offset, measurable and refreshable) while a slope error enters only as δA × δ⟨ℰ⟩ — first order in the deviation. This is why the learned/approximated content is deliberately confined to Poff and the response stays analytic.

(e) Ion closure. The ionic channel keeps the full nonlinear form of Eqs. (51), (53) evaluated at the averaged potential:

ion closure⟨ρion⟩(z)=nmax⟨Sion⟩(z)ρ~ion(⟨φ⟩(z))

This closure is benign — ions live where φ is laterally flat — and it never failed: ρion errors are 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ e/Å³ in every variant including the naive one (§3). The cavities ⟨Sdiel⟩, ⟨Sion⟩ must be built in 3-D from ne and then averaged: the erfc/ln nonlinearity of Eqs. (1)–(2) does not commute with averaging.

(f) The 1-D equation actually solved (Newton iteration on 300–500 z-points, dipole-correction loop outside, ~0.03 s):

1-D PB−ε04πd2⟨φ⟩dz2=⟨ρsol⟩−ddz[wb∗(A⟨ℰz⟩+Poff)]+nmax⟨Sion⟩ρ~ion(⟨φ⟩)

Inputs: ⟨ρsol⟩ and A, ⟨Sion⟩ follow the current model density (analytic, differentiable); Poff carries the lateral-structure memory (anchor, or prior + learned residual). ⟨ℰz⟩ and ⟨Pz⟩ are solved for, exactly as φ, p are in the 3-D code.

(g) The no-solve prior for Poff. The lateral field structure is dominated by the analytically known solute potential φsol, so the covariance can be estimated without solving anything:

screened-vacuum priorPoffprior(z)=ε04π⟨δχ(ℰscr)·δℰzscr⟩,ℰscr≡−(wb∗∇φsol)ε(r),ε(r)=1+4πε0χ0(r)

Inputs: ne (→ cavity → χ₀ → ε) and the solute charges (→ φsol) — no polarization state, no solve; the response χ and the mean slope A = ⟨χ(ℰscr)⟩ are evaluated at the same screened field (one consistent pairing). Measured accuracy 1.6% of the Poff peak (§5); the residual — the self-consistent interface screening the local division cannot capture — is the learned target.

The mapping is superlinear: the potential is a double integral of the bound charge, so systematically-biased Poff errors accumulate. Working requirement: Poff to ~5×10⁻⁴ absolute for solver-grade φ (~10⁻² eV); ~10⁻³ for training-grade. This is the accuracy bar any learned or approximated Poff must clear — it is a hard target, not a soft one. Each row's error is measured against the Poff paired with that row's A source (peak 0.338 for the formula-A rows, 0.304 for the screened-vacuum pairing).

6Poff is geometry-dominated

Fixed geometry (cal_1), density coefficients captured from models at three training stages, one accurate 3-D solve each:

epoch 25 vs epoch 835.89 → 6.037.0e-41.12%
epoch 9 (near-untrained) vs 833.86 → 6.039.5e-41.53%

The density changes enormously; Poff moves ~1%. Poff is set by the geometry (where the cavity is), not by the fine structure of the density. Two consequences: labels computed once per structure stay valid across a whole training run, and a learned Poff(geometry) is an interpolation task, not a moving target.

7Where this lands

One uniform, stateless pipeline for training and MD — the same inputs in both (no lagged refresh anywhere):

  1. Labels (offline, once): one 3-D solve per training geometry (320 × 2.4 s ≈ 13 min) → exact (geometry → Poff, A, s̄ion) pairs. The 3-D solver's only remaining role.
  2. Closure heads: small regression heads supervised only by these labels (never by the potential loss — no fudge channel), pre-trained to the §4 accuracy bar, then in the loop.
  3. Every step (training and MD identically): model forward → heads predict Poff/A/s̄ion → differentiable 1-D nonlinear solve (~0.03 s, 300–500 points, ions fully nonlinear). Fully differentiable, no state.
Open question, and the route's single remaining risk: cross-geometry learnability — can a head predict Poff to ~10⁻³ on held-out structures? Everything else on this page is measured.
