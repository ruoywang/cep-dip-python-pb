PB Grid Convergence on the Training Cell — 0.15 Å It Is

How coarse a grid can the per-structure PB solve use inside MACE training? Study on a real training-set GCE structure (cal_1: NiN slab + water, 45 Å cell, VASPsol reference fields, native grid 168×168×500): the CHGCAR density spectrally restricted to eight integer-argument grids, each solved with the validated torch backend (5 dipole fixsteps, tol 1e-3) and compared term-by-term against the reference. Neither solvent-charge channel converges alone — the ionic and bound (dielectric polarization) dipole terms drift by ≥0.5 V already at 0.15 — but their errors cancel: the combined solvent term is accurate to ≤0.02 V through 0.15 Å and degrades quickly beyond (+0.13 V at 0.20, +2.7 V at 0.25). Decision: include_bound + grid_spacing 0.15.

The Cancellation That Sets the Grid

Error of each solvent dipole-potential term vs the native 0.089 Å grid, against effective grid spacing. The ionic term drifts negative, the bound term positive; the sum stays within ±0.02 V through the 0.15 grid, is marginal at 0.20 (+0.13 V), and breaks away beyond.

Why they cancel: the total screening-charge dipole is pinned by the electrostatics of the converged potential; discretization mostly reshuffles charge between the ionic and polarization channels. Feeding MACE the combined profile ρion(z)+ρb(z) is therefore both more physical (the −6.3 V screening dipole was missing from the ion-only layer) and numerically forgiving — --solvent_pb_include_bound, now the default.

What Happens on a Coarse Grid — Both Charges and the Potential

Plane-averaged profiles over the full 45 Å cell (physics sign). The two charge channels are equal parts of one solvent layer: μtotal = μion + μb. On coarse grids the ionic layer is pulled toward the slab and compressed; the bound charge's dipolar structure at the cavity edges is smeared out; and the electrostatic potential — aligned at the right vacuum plateau (z ∈ [41.5, 43.5], the training's potential_1d_profile_align: upper convention) — loses the double-layer drop. Pick the grids to compare:

ρion(z) — ionic charge
ρb(z) — bound (polarization) charge
φ(z) — electrostatic potential, vacuum-aligned, full cell
About the visible sawtooth: the jagged near-grid oscillation on coarse grids is aliasing of the under-resolved cavity-kernel transition (width ≈0.21 Å). Its amplitude is non-monotonic in spacing — it is an interference effect over the whole 3D cavity surface, quasi-random between grid shapes — and it largely integrates out of the dipole observables: visual smoothness is not an accuracy signal. Profile smoothing was tested and rejected: a periodic Gaussian smooth transports the boundary-reaching ringing tail across the cell edge and corrupts μb (combined-term error 10× worse), while non-periodic smoothing is accuracy-neutral at best. The systematic edge displacement — the actual failure mode — is untouched by smoothing.

Findings & Decision

  • Neither channel converges alone; the sum does. The prime suspect for the drift is exp_kernel_g: the cavity kernel's transition width A_K/SIGMA_K ≈ 0.21 Å is under-resolved beyond ≈0.15–0.19 Å spacing and, unlike the Gaussian kernel, it is normalized analytically rather than on the grid.
  • The bound charge belongs in the solvent layer. Its dipole term is −6.3 V against the ionic +22.2 V — leaving it out both discards that physics and forfeits the ion/bound error cancellation that makes a coarse grid usable at all (ion-only would need ≤0.10 Å for 0.1 V-level accuracy).
  • The cliff position is cell-dependent. An earlier run of the same protocol on the cal_18 reference structure (35 Å cell) put the cliff one notch later (+0.014 V at 0.186 Å effective vs +0.129 V here): the edge-displacement error depends on how the cavity edges fall on the grid, which varies between cells and between water configurations. Across the 320 training structures the per-structure error scatters within an envelope — the grid must be chosen where that envelope is tight.
  • Decision: training runs use --solvent_model pb --solvent_pb_include_bound --solvent_pb_grid_spacing 0.15 (100×100×300; +0.017 V on cal_1; ~2.5 s per structure in the 2-fixstep training protocol, ≈15 min of PB per epoch). A multi-structure envelope check at 0.15 is the natural follow-up before long training runs.
  • Future lead (not done): grid-renormalizing / band-limiting the exponential cavity kernel could push the usable spacing coarser and remove the sawtooth at its source; it touches pb.py and therefore requires the cal_18 gate rerun.
